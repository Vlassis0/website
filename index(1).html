What you're actually getting

Inside every build

Whether it's a free grant or an assisted build, every machine goes through the same standard before it reaches you.

Hardware

Donated & sourced parts

Cases, drives, memory, and boards come from community and business donations, plus parts we buy when a build needs something specific.

Testing

Burn-in & stress tested

Every machine runs a multi-hour stress test before handoff, so drives, memory, and cooling are checked under real load, not just powered on.

Software

Ready on day one

A clean OS install with browser, office software, and security updates already in place — no setup required when you get it home.

Warranty

90-day support window

If something goes wrong within 90 days of pickup or delivery, bring it back and we'll repair or replace it at no extra cost.

Fit

Sized to the task

We ask what you'll use the computer for — job applications, schoolwork, remote work — and match the build to that, not the other way around.

Sustainability

Fewer machines in landfill

Parts that don't make it into a working build are stripped for components or recycled responsibly rather than thrown out.

The process

From application to power-on

The same four steps, whichever path you're on — most applicants hear back within a week and have a machine within two to three.

01

Questions

Before you apply

The most common things people ask us. Reach out if yours isn't covered here.

How do you decide who qualifies for a free computer?

Path A is prioritized for people currently without income — job seekers, students, and families in need. We ask about your situation on the application so we can match you to the right path; there's no credit check and no hidden qualification test.

What does "cost assistance" actually mean for Path B?

If you have a job, we still don't charge full retail. Pricing is scaled to what you can reasonably afford, and we offer payment plans instead of asking for the full amount up front.

What condition are the computers in?

Refurbished, not used-as-is. Every machine is cleaned, tested under load, and repaired or replaced part-by-part where needed before it goes out, and it comes with a 90-day support window.

Can I choose what kind of computer I get?

You can tell us what you need it for — job applications, schoolwork, remote work — and we'll match a build to that. We work with what's been donated or sourced, so exact specs vary, but the fit to your use case doesn't.

Can I donate a computer instead of applying for one?

Yes — email us at madguy8288@gmail.com and we'll let you know what we're currently able to take and how to get it to us.
